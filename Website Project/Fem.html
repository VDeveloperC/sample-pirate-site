Anne Bonny and Mary Read

Anne Bonny and Mary Read were cross-dressing pirates that ended up becoming one of the most fiercest female pirates of the Golden Age of Piracy in the 18th century. Although there are many stories of their adventures together as pirates the most famous one would be that they both defeated Calico Jack. The two of them overpowered the crew and Jack which resulted in him surrending. The men ended up rotting in prision after that waiting for their sentences, while Anne and Mary got away by telling the privateers that they were pregnant and were given a stay of execution. Anne Bonny and Mary Read's legacy lived on and marked their place in history as the two most famous female pirates ever known, and inspired many female pirates.

Anne Bonny was Irish but as a pirate she operated in the Caribbean. She ended up marrying a pirate named James Bonny and eventually had an affair with the famous pirate Calico Jack (John Rackham) and ended up joining his crew which is where she met Mark Read who was secretly Mary Read and the two ended up becoming very close. After defeating Calico Jack and being spared from execution Anne was released from prision and disappeared.

Mary Read was born in Englash but also ended up opperating in the Carribean as a pirate. Before becoming a pirate she was first living as a man and joined the British army in order to fight in the War of The Spanish Succesion. After the war she got married and settled down as a woman but ended up cross-dressing again as a man after the death of her husband and left to sail the West Indies. She became a pirate after her ship was taken by pirates and willingly joined the crew of Calico Jack. Saddly marry died in prision from a violent fever after being spared from exectuion.
